Televisions & Energy Efficiency

Key Facts about TV Power Consumption in Australia

  • New televisions usually use more energy than a refrigerator when on for many hours.
  • Many Australian homes have 2+ TVs, multiplying energy use.
  • LED/LCD TVs are significantly more efficient than older plasma models.
  • Typical on-mode power: 50–150 W for mid-size modern TVs (varies greatly by size & brightness).
  • Standby power should be < 0.5–1 W on efficient models.
  • Energy Rating Label assumes 10 hours on + 14 hours standby per day.
  • Higher star ratings (4–6+ stars common on good models) = noticeably lower bills.
  • Larger screens (e.g. 75"+) use substantially more power than smaller ones.

Quick tips: Lower brightness, use eco/energy-saving mode, switch off at the wall (not just standby), choose smaller size if possible.
