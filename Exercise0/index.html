Understanding Appliance Energy Consumption in Australia

Australian households use a significant amount of electricity on appliances and home entertainment. Choosing energy-efficient products helps reduce bills and environmental impact.

  • Appliances account for around 30% of typical home energy use.
  • Home entertainment equipment (including TVs) contributes ~5% of average household energy consumption.
  • Modern LED TVs are far more efficient than older plasma models.
  • Standby power ("vampire power") can account for up to 10% of some electricity bills.
  • All TVs sold in Australia must display an Energy Rating Label (1–10 stars). More stars = lower running costs.
  • A large inefficient TV can use as much energy per year as a medium-sized refrigerator.

Tip: Always check the annual energy consumption (kWh/year) on the label and compare models of similar size.
