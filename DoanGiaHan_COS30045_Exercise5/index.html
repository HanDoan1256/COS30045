TV Energy Consumption and Electricity Price Visualisation

Scatter Plot: Energy Consumption vs Star Rating
This chart explores the relationship between the energy star rating of televisions and their annual energy consumption. Each point represents an individual TV model. Generally, TVs with higher star ratings are expected to consume less electricity.
Note: Lower energy consumption combined with higher star ratings indicates better energy efficiency.
Donut Chart: Energy Consumption by Screen Technology
This donut chart shows how energy consumption differs between screen technologies such as LED, LCD, and OLED. The values represent the average labelled energy consumption across all television sizes.
Note: Screen technology can significantly influence power usage due to differences in backlighting and display design.
Bar Chart: Energy Consumption for 55-inch TVs
This chart compares the average annual energy consumption of 55-inch TVs across different screen technologies. By focusing on one size category, the chart makes technology differences easier to compare.
Note: Larger TVs generally consume more power, so comparing within the same size provides a fair comparison.
Line Chart: Australian Electricity Spot Prices (1998–2024)
This chart shows how electricity spot prices have changed over time. The line represents the yearly average electricity price across the dataset.
Note: Long-term trends in electricity prices may be influenced by market changes, energy policy, and demand fluctuations.
