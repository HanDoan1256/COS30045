TV Energy Consumption and Electricity Price Visualisation

This webpage presents multiple data visualisations exploring television energy consumption and electricity market trends. Using D3.js, different chart types are used to highlight relationships in the dataset and make the data easier to interpret.

---

Scatter Plot: Energy Consumption vs Energy Star Rating

This scatter plot shows the relationship between the energy star rating of televisions and their annual energy consumption. Each point represents a television model. The chart helps identify whether televisions with higher star ratings tend to consume less electricity.

---

Donut Chart: Energy Consumption by Screen Technology

This donut chart illustrates how average energy consumption varies across different television screen technologies such as LED, LCD, and OLED. By aggregating data across all TV sizes, the chart highlights which technologies generally consume more electricity.

---

Bar Chart: Energy Consumption for 55-inch TVs

This bar chart compares the average yearly energy consumption of 55-inch televisions across different screen technologies. By focusing on a single screen size, the chart provides a clearer comparison of how technology affects energy efficiency.

---

Line Chart: Australian Electricity Spot Prices (1998–2024)

This line chart visualises the trend of electricity spot prices over time. It helps reveal long-term changes in electricity costs and allows viewers to observe periods of price increases or stability across the dataset.
